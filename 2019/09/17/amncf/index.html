Attentive Multimodal Neural Collaborative Filtering for Group Recommendation

1. Abstract

In recent years, group recommender which aims to provide recommendation to a group of users has attracted a lot of concerns. Meanwhile, the latest deep learning-based approaches have shown promising results for the group recommendation task. However, existing works mainly focus on the interactions and ratings data, while studies using multimodal information for group recommendation are rarely explored. In order to make better use of the multimodal information and improve the group recommendation result, we propose a new model AMNCF (short for “Attentive Multimodal Neural Collaborative Filtering for Group Recommendation”). Specifically, in our model, we use HAN and RAN to extract item’s important visual and textual features, respectively. Then we concatenate them with the item’s id embedding to generate the original multimodal embedding of item. Next, self-attention mechanism is implemented to automatically assign weight to each modality, and then multiply each modality with its corresponding weight to form the final multimodal embedding of item. On the other hand, we also design attention network to distinguish the importance of contributions of group members. Finally, we jointly train group recommendation and personalized recommendation tasks on the same NCF framework. Experiments on two real world datasets show that our proposed method outperforms the baseline methods. Specifically, the hit rates of our proposed model on both datasets are improved by about 10\% in both group recommendation and personalized recommendation tasks. The ablation studies also show that the multimodal information and intra-/inter- attention networks have improved the performance of the group recommendation system to some extent. We will release our source code at https://github.com/ZSCDumin/AMNCF.

2. Datasets

We constructed the datasets by crawling data from IMDb and BookCrossing. (1) IMDb is a movie website where users can not only watch movies which they are interested in, but also they can make comments or rating for these films they watched. (2) BookCrossing is a book website where users can not only buy books which they like, but also they can make comments or rating for these books they bought.
